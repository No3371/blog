I've been a royal GitKraken user for about 2 years... I love the UI/UX, love the experience, I recommended it to a lot of people... well, it's a lot to me, I don't really have friends, you need to understand.

But! Here comes the "but", GitKraken is notorious for its performance issue with big repo... [for at least 10 years](https://github.com/git-cola/git-cola/issues/48). I work in a not-so-small company that we'd deal with huge projects, like tens of or over 100 mbs of **scripts** in the projects. It's really a PITA for a long time to work with GitKraken for these repos.

It just doesn't work, nothting can justify the fact that it needs several seconds to display one single file... I mean, what the fuck? Even NotePad can do better then that, reading a file has nothing to do with git, right?

In the past, I sometimes searched for a alternative git client that looks good, feels good like GitKraken, for me, UI/UX is important, or I'd rather to mess with git-cli training myself to become a git-guru. There's actually none, I must say, none of them has UI so polished as GitKraken, so I had never really given a try to any.

Finally, I got pissed off today, I went to the official [GUI Clients](https://git-scm.com/downloads/guis) page and did some shopping, eventually settled in Fork. On it's official page it's titled "Fast and Friendly" and it's no advertisement but just honest bio, even the UI/UX is much better then I imagined!

BTW, there's something calls Ungit which is a web-based git client written in node js and that thing runs faster then GitKraken, man.
